# Fairshot — Marketing & Messaging Guide

> **Brand:** Fairshot  
> **Parent:** Soft Tooling  
> **Tagline:** *We run your job search. You pay when it works.*  
> **Tone:** Warm, confident, quietly intelligent. Never salesy. Never apologetic.

---

## 1. Brand Voice

### We sound like:
A thoughtful friend who happens to be excellent at job searching. Someone who believes in you without being condescending. Direct without being blunt.

### We never sound like:
- A corporate HR tool
- A hustle-bro productivity guru
- A therapist
- A resume robot

### Voice principles:
1. **Specific over generic** — "12 quality applications" not "apply to more jobs"
2. **Human over machine** — "We reframe what you actually did" not "AI-optimized resume"
3. **Quiet over loud** — No exclamation points. No ALL CAPS. No urgency.
4. **Honest over hype** — If we don't do something, we say so.

---

## 2. Messaging Hierarchy

### Level 1: Headline (what we do)
> We run your job search. You pay when it works.

### Level 2: Subhead (the problem we solve)
> No resume scoring. No shame spirals. No 47-step templates.

### Level 3: Body (how we do it)
> You tell us where you want to go. We find worth-your-time roles, tailor your application for each one, and track what happens. You only pay when you get results.

### Level 4: Proof (what makes us different)
- No scoring. We reframe, not criticize.
- Real customization per job, not mail merge.
- Milestone pricing: pay for outcomes, not subscriptions.

## 5. The "What We Say vs. What They Say" Framework

Use this contrast pattern across all marketing:

| What other tools say | What Fairshot says |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| "Your resume scores 42/100. Weak action verbs." | "Here's a stronger framing of what you actually did." |
| "You applied to 200 jobs this week!" | "12 quality applications to roles that fit. 3 responses." |
| "Your subscription renews tomorrow." | "You haven't paid us in three months. That's fine." |
| "Try harder." | "You're closer than you think." |

---

## 6. Pricing Messaging

### Frame it as:
- **Milestone pricing** — not "pay per application"
- **Progress-based** — not "subscription-free"
- **You control the pace** — not "no commitment"

### Avoid:
- "Cheap" / "affordable" / "discount"
- "Unlimited" (we don't do unlimited)
- "Free trial" (there's no subscription to trial)

### Pricing page copy:
> Most tools charge you to *try.* We charge you when something actually happens. Each milestone unlocks the next. No subscriptions. No cancellation anxiety.

## 8. Visual Identity Notes

- **Palette:** Warm cream background (#f5f4f2), deep charcoal text (#3f3a33), teal accent (#0f8a7d)
- **Typography:** Newsreader (serif display) + Inter (sans body)
- **Mood:** Editorial magazine, not SaaS landing page. Think Monocle meets wellness.
- **Photography:** Real humans in real workspaces. No stock-photo handshakes.
- **Icons:** Minimal. No emoji. No generic feature icons.

---

## 9. Launch Sequence (Suggested)

1. **Waitlist landing** (`fairshot.html`) — collect emails, explain philosophy
2. **Beta cohort** — 50 users, Milestone 01 + 02 only
3. **Case studies** — 3–5 before/after stories with real results
4. **Public launch** — all milestones, PR push around "anti-resume-scoring"
5. **Content engine** — blog: "Your resume is a story," "Why we don't score," "The spray-and-pray lie"
